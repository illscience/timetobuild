# Review Output Format

Use this structure for all deck reviews. Adapt section depth based on the deck — short sections for areas that are fine, detailed sections for areas with meaningful feedback.

---

## Deck Review: [Company Name or "Untitled" if not clear]

**Perspective:** [Firm] | [Fund, if applicable] | [Stage]
**Deck length:** [X slides]

---

### Overall Assessment

1-2 sentences: What is this company, and would this investor take a meeting based on this deck? Be direct.

---

### Slide-by-Slide Feedback

For each slide (or logical group of slides):
- **Slide N — [Slide Title/Topic]**: What works, what doesn't, what's missing. Be specific — reference actual content on the slide.

Skip slides that are fine as-is. Focus your energy on the slides that matter most.

---

### Strengths — What Would Excite This Investor

Bulleted list. Each strength should reference the specific evaluation criteria it maps to (e.g., "Strong domain depth — the founding team's background in X directly addresses [firm]'s emphasis on idea maze navigation").

---

### Concerns — What Would Give This Investor Pause

Bulleted list. Each concern should reference the specific evaluation criteria (e.g., "No evidence of talent magnetism — [firm] looks for founders who can materialize labor, and the team slide doesn't address hiring ability or early team formation").

---

### Missing Elements

What slides, data, or narrative elements are absent that this firm/stage would expect? Be specific about what to add and where in the deck.

---

### Founder Signal Read

*Include this section for seed and pre-seed stage reviews only.*

Based on what the deck reveals (not speculates), assess the founder against the firm's founder evaluation criteria. What signals come through? What's missing? What questions would the investor want to ask in a meeting to fill the gaps?

---

### Suggested Narrative Arc

If the deck's story structure could be improved, suggest a reordering or reframing. Provide a specific slide-by-slide outline of the improved narrative.

If the current arc works well, say so briefly and skip the detailed restructure.

---

### Verdict: [Pass / Soft Pass / Take the Meeting / Strong Interest]

- **Pass**: This deck would not get a meeting at this firm in its current form.
- **Soft Pass**: Interesting elements but significant gaps. Worth a warm intro but not a cold outreach.
- **Take the Meeting**: This deck earns a first meeting. Note what the meeting would focus on.
- **Strong Interest**: This deck would generate genuine excitement. Note what would need to be true for a term sheet.

Calibrate to the specific firm's bar and the stage. A "Take the Meeting" at Sequoia is different from a "Take the Meeting" at a seed fund.
