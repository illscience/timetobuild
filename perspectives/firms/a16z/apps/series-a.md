# a16z Apps Fund — Series A Evaluation

At Series A, the bar shifts from founder potential to founder + proof. The question is no longer "is this a big idea with the right person?" but "is this working, and can it scale?"

## What Must Be Proven

### Product-Market Fit
- Evidence of genuine PMF, not forced growth. Are customers pulling or is the company pushing?
- Retention cohorts that flatten (not just grow-then-churn)
- Organic growth or efficient paid acquisition — evidence the market wants this
- Customer quotes, case studies, or NPS data that show love, not just usage

### Unit Economics
- Clear LTV/CAC framework, even if early
- Path to positive unit economics at scale, with credible assumptions
- For enterprise: ACV, sales cycle length, expansion revenue
- For consumer: engagement depth, monetization per user, viral coefficient

### Repeatable Growth
- Is there a scalable acquisition channel that works without the founder closing every deal?
- Evidence of a sales motion (enterprise) or growth loop (consumer) that can be systematized
- Early signs that growth compounds rather than requiring linear effort

### Operational Readiness
- Team has scaled beyond founders — key functional hires in place
- Evidence of process and systems, not just hustle
- Clear org plan for the next 12-18 months of hiring

## Metrics That Matter

### SaaS / Enterprise Apps
- ARR and ARR growth rate (MoM and YoY)
- Net revenue retention (>120% is strong)
- Gross margin (>70% for software)
- CAC payback period
- Logo retention and expansion rates
- Pipeline and win rates

### Consumer Apps
- DAU/MAU ratio (engagement depth)
- D1/D7/D30 retention
- Cohort revenue curves
- Viral coefficient / organic growth %
- Time spent / sessions per user
- Monetization rate and ARPU

### AI Applications (Series A specific)
- All of the above, plus:
- Model cost as % of revenue (and trajectory)
- Accuracy/quality metrics and improvement over time
- Data flywheel evidence — does more usage = better product?
- Customer switching costs — how embedded is the AI in the workflow?

## Deck Expectations at Series A

- **20-25 slides** is appropriate. More data-dense than seed.
- **Must include**: Problem, Solution, Market, Product demo or screenshots, Traction (detailed), Unit Economics, Team, Competition, GTM strategy, Financial projections, Ask + use of funds
- **Narrative shift**: From "imagine what this could be" to "look at what this IS and here's why it scales"
- **Design quality**: Should be professional but substance >> style. Data clarity matters more than aesthetics.

## a16z-Specific Series A Priorities

- **Platform potential**: At Series A, a16z wants to see the path from product to platform. Can this become an ecosystem?
- **Leverage the a16z machine**: Founders should show awareness of how a16z's operational support accelerates growth — GTM help, talent network, enterprise partnerships
- **Category creation**: Is this company defining a new category, or competing in an established one? a16z prefers category creators.
- **AI thesis alignment**: How does this company fit the "AI is eating software" thesis? Is AI a core differentiator or a feature?

## Founder Assessment (Still Matters, But Different)

At Series A, the founder assessment is validated by results:
- Did they materialize labor? Look at the team they've built.
- Did they fast-boil? Look at the traction timeline.
- Domain depth? Evident in the product and market understanding.
- Decision velocity? Evident in the speed of iteration and pivots.

The question shifts from "can they do it?" to "have they done it, and can they keep doing it at scale?"
