# a16z Growth Fund — Investment Criteria

a16z Growth invests in market-leading companies with ambitious visions for emerging categories in technology. We look for bold founders building companies with differentiated products, compelling unit economics, and a breakthrough view of their opportunity in the largest markets of the future.

We have invested as little as $50 million and more than $1 billion in our portfolio companies.

Below are six criteria that the Growth fund assesses each prospective investment on. While we like businesses who can check all of the boxes, we are in the business of exceptions — so from time to time we do not require all.

---

## 1. Differentiated value proposition that tackles a critical pain point in a transformative way

- We like companies that solve a mission critical problem ("need to have" not "nice to have").
- We gravitate toward technology-driven businesses where new technology unlocks a fundamentally new, not just incrementally better, experience. Put another way, we prefer "what" businesses (novel capabilities made possible by new technology) over "how" businesses.
- The company delivers a beloved customer experience, such that the market is demanding more of its product. There is market pull, not push.

## 2. Durable competitive moats

- Many companies are growing at an exceptional clip (and we want to meet them!), but we look for defensibility that compounds over time. We care about why a company is winning today and why that will be the case in 10+ years.
- We look for durable sources of defensibility: network effects, unique technology/IP, economies of scale, brand, high switching costs, and bargaining power/distribution control.

## 3. World-class team

- We partner with exceptional founding teams who demonstrated strong execution and have the ambition and capability to lead a large public company.
- The company has a track record of meeting or exceeding product roadmap milestones and financial forecasts.
- The CEO tends to be technical and materializes capital and labor. Top executives want to work at the company.

## 4. Large and growing market

- We invest in the most important markets of the future. The market is often already large, but what matters most is where it will be in 10+ years.
- The industries we back have strong structural tailwinds and the potential to support companies worth tens of billions, hopefully hundreds of billions, and potentially trillions of market cap.
- We put significant weight on market leadership. We invest in companies that are the clear emerging category winner, or where we have strong conviction that they can displace the current leading player.

## 5. Recurring and high-margin business model with attractive unit economics

- We expect demonstrated strong demand and product-market fit translating to exceptional revenue growth that will compound at a high rate for many years.
- We seek high engagement, high retention products and services as proxies for mission criticality and future revenue predictability.
- Recurring revenue whether subscription or naturally reoccurring, such as transactional or consumption.
- We prefer high gross margin businesses as an indicator of future cash flow potential.
- We expect to see operating leverage as the business scales.
- We look for attractive unit economics — we like to see high returns on sales & marketing dollars as a function of gross profit and retention.

## 6. Product vision to enter large adjacent markets with a unique wedge

- We seek companies that have a first product that is both exceptional on its own and a beachhead for a much broader suite of offerings.
- We look for a credible, specific roadmap into adjacent markets with early proof points to demonstrate this company is uniquely positioned to win those adjacencies.
