## Core Thesis

a16z was founded on the belief that **founding CEOs outperform professional CEOs over the long term**, and that a venture firm designed to help founding CEOs succeed would build the best companies. The firm invests in problems, not products — if the problem doesn't matter, neither does the solution.

The firm's worldview is built on two foundational essays:

**"Software is eating the world"** — Software companies are systematically displacing traditional businesses across every industry. Six decades into the computer revolution, the enabling technologies (broadband, cloud, mobile) have matured enough to make this transformation possible at global scale. Companies built on software have real defensibility and margin characteristics that justify aggressive investment.

**"It's time to build"** — The central failure of modern institutions is not a lack of money or foresight, but a failure of *will* and *action*. The question a16z asks of every founder: **"What are you building?"** The firm backs people who are building things that matter — not protecting incumbents or optimizing the status quo, but constructing the foundations of what comes next.

## Current Focus

The latest evolution of the thesis: **AI is eating software.** AI's central role across the portfolio means every company should have a clear view on how AI impacts their market — either as a tailwind they're riding or a disruption they're navigating.

From the $15B raise: a16z believes "America wins the next 100 years of technology" is essential to global prosperity. The firm invests in foundational technologies (AI, crypto), human flourishing applications (biology, health, defense, education, entertainment), and ensuring American institutions leverage these technologies. Technology advancement directly correlates with human opportunity.

## What a16z Cares About

- **Problem significance**: Is this a real problem that matters to real people or businesses?
- **Founders who build**: "What are you building?" is the defining question. a16z backs founders with the will to act, not just the insight to see.
- **Addressable reality**: Market sizing grounded in reality, not fantasy. a16z sees through market fluff.
- **Core innovation clarity**: One thing that makes the approach different from the status quo. Not a feature list — the singular insight.
- **Platform ambition**: Can this become a platform, not just a product?
- **Network effects and compounding moats**: Does the product get better as it scales?
- **Founding CEO strength**: The firm was built to support founder-CEOs. They look for founders who can grow into the CEO role, not founders who will need to be replaced.

## Fund Structure

a16z operates distinct funds with different investment criteria:

- **Apps**: Consumer and enterprise applications, AI applications, creator tools, social, gaming, fintech
- **Infrastructure**: Cloud, distributed systems, AI infrastructure, data platforms, developer tools, networking
- **Crypto**: Blockchain protocols, DeFi, token economies, Web3 applications
- **Bio**: Life sciences, healthcare, biotech
- **American Dynamism**: Defense, government, public safety, transportation, housing, education — companies building for sectors critical to American strength
- **Speedrun**: Early-stage accelerator for pre-seed and seed companies. Invests across sectors with a cohort-based model, focus on supporting new teams

Each fund has its own evaluation lens. Use the fund-specific perspective files for detailed criteria.
