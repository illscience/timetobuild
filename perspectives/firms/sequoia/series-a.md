# Sequoia Capital — Series A Evaluation

At Series A, Sequoia is looking for proof that the seed thesis was right. The four core questions remain, but the bar for answers is higher — backed by data, not just conviction.

## Elevated Bar on the 10-Point Rubric

### 1-4. Purpose, Problem, Solution, Why Now
- These should be sharper than at seed, refined by customer feedback and market experience
- The "eureka moment" should now be validated, not theoretical
- "Why now" should have additional evidence from the time since founding

### 5. Market Potential
- Bottom-up market sizing required, informed by actual customer data
- Evidence of the specific segment you're winning and why
- Expansion path to adjacent segments should be clear

### 6. Competition
- Must reflect real competitive encounters, not theoretical positioning
- Win/loss data or qualitative competitive intelligence
- Evidence of a sustainable moat forming

### 7. Business Model
- Unit economics must be demonstrated, not projected
- Clear understanding of CAC, LTV, payback period, margins
- Business model should be validated by actual revenue, not assumptions

### 8. Team
- Evidence of hiring ability — who have you attracted and why?
- Key functional roles filled beyond just founders
- Advisory board and investor quality as social proof

### 9. Financials
- 18-24 month historical data
- Forward projections grounded in actuals, not hockey sticks
- Clear path to key financial milestones

### 10. Vision
- Vision should be more concrete — informed by what you've learned
- Product roadmap for the next 18 months should be credible
- Platform or ecosystem ambition should be emerging

## Series A Must-Haves

### Traction Data
- Revenue or usage growth (MoM, QoQ)
- Cohort retention curves
- Key engagement metrics
- Customer concentration risk

### Product-Market Fit Evidence
- Customer testimonials or case studies
- Organic growth or inbound demand
- Expansion within existing accounts
- Low churn / high retention

### Go-to-Market Clarity
- Defined ICP (ideal customer profile)
- Repeatable sales motion or growth loop
- Channel strategy with early evidence
- CAC by channel

### Operational Maturity
- Defined processes for key functions
- Metrics and reporting infrastructure
- Hiring plan tied to milestones
- Board governance in place or planned

## Deck Expectations

- **20-25 slides** — more data-dense than seed
- Opening slides should still pass the "3-minute test"
- Data should be presented honestly — no cherry-picked timeframes or misleading charts
- Appendix slides for detailed metrics are expected and welcomed
- Design should be professional but substance-first
