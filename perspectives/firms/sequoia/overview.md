# Sequoia Capital — General Investment Philosophy

Source: [Writing a Business Plan](https://sequoiacap.com/article/writing-a-business-plan/)

## Core Evaluation

Sequoia evaluates every opportunity against four questions that must be answered in the first 3 minutes:

1. **Can I understand this?** — If the company can't be explained simply and quickly, it's a red flag.
2. **Is this a big market?** — Sequoia needs billion-dollar outcome potential.
3. **Why will this team win?** — Ideas are cheap; execution is everything.
4. **Why now?** — Timing kills more startups than bad ideas.

## Philosophy

> "Ideas, clarity of thinking, and scope of ambition" over polished presentations.

Sequoia values structured thinking presented clearly. Every slide has a job — if it doesn't do that job, cut it. Less is more.

## What Sequoia Looks For

- **Company purpose in a single declarative sentence**: If you can't do this, your thinking isn't clear enough
- **Problem-first framing**: The problem must be described before the solution
- **"Eureka moment"**: What's the unique insight that makes this solution possible?
- **"Nature hates a vacuum"**: If this is a real opportunity, why hasn't it been built before? What changed?
- **Scope of ambition**: What will this look like in 5 years? Sequoia wants to see the long-term vision, not just the initial product.

## What Kills a Deal

- Can't explain the company simply in the first slide
- Market opportunity isn't credibly large
- No clear answer to "why now?"
- Team doesn't have relevant domain experience
- Deck is overdesigned or tries to obscure lack of substance with polish
- No vision beyond the immediate product
