# Universal Pitch Deck Review Framework

This is the baseline evaluation applied to every deck regardless of firm, fund, or stage. Firm-specific perspectives layer additional criteria on top.

## Structural Completeness

Every pitch deck should address these core elements (weight and depth varies by stage):

1. **Problem**: Is there a real, significant problem being solved?
2. **Solution**: Is the solution clear and compelling?
3. **Why Now**: What changed to make this possible/necessary today?
4. **Market**: Is the opportunity large enough to build a venture-scale business?
5. **Product**: What does the product do and how does it work?
6. **Traction**: What evidence exists that this is working? (stage-dependent)
7. **Business Model**: How does or will this make money?
8. **Team**: Why is this the team to win?
9. **Competition**: Who else is trying to solve this and why will you win?
10. **Ask**: What are you raising and what will you do with it?

## Narrative Quality

- **Clarity**: Can someone understand the company in under 60 seconds from the first few slides?
- **Flow**: Does each slide logically build on the previous one?
- **Conciseness**: Are slides doing one job each, or trying to do too much?
- **Data presentation**: Are metrics and charts clear and honestly presented?
- **Visual quality**: Is the design clean and professional without being over-produced?

## Common Red Flags (any stage)

- No clear problem statement or a solution looking for a problem
- Market sizing that relies on "if we capture X% of a huge market"
- No competitive landscape or claiming "no competitors"
- Team slide with no relevant domain experience
- Ask slide with vague use of funds
- Too many slides (>20 for seed, >25 for Series A)
- Walls of text instead of clear visuals and key points
- Inconsistent or missing metrics
- Buzzword-heavy language that obscures rather than clarifies

## Common Green Flags (any stage)

- Opens with a clear, specific problem statement grounded in real experience
- Demonstrates unique insight or unfair advantage
- Shows evidence of customer love (quotes, NPS, retention data)
- Team has direct, relevant experience in the problem domain
- Clear, honest competitive positioning
- Specific, credible use of funds tied to milestones
- Deck tells a story you want to keep reading
